WHY DISASTER RESPONSE

Disaster response is the second phase of the disaster management cycle. It consists of a number of elements, for example warning/evacuation, search and rescue, providing immediate assistance, assessing damage, continuing assistance and the immediate restoration or construction of infrastructure. The aim of emergency response is to provide immediate assistance to maintain life, improve health and support the morale of the affected population. Such assistance may range from providing specific but limited aid, such as assisting refugees with transport, temporary shelter, and food to establishing semi-permanent settlement in camps and other locations.

Projects

Assam Floods

Several charity foundations actively supported Assam flood relief. Walmart & Flipkart Foundations provided food, hygiene kits, and healthcare through Doctors For You. AID India ran 30 health camps in flood-hit villages. Ajmal Foundation donated ₹10 lakh to the CM Relief Fund. Smile Foundation and Maarif Foundation also supplied essentials, temporary shelter, and education support.

Jammu & Kashmir Floods

During the 2014 Jammu & Kashmir floods, several charity foundations stepped in. Khalsa Aid provided food, clean water, and rescue help. Amma’s foundation offered a $4 million relief package, including homes and medical aid. ActionAid distributed hygiene kits, set up toilets, and supported education. The World Federation donated emergency food supplies, while the Red Cross supplied medical and rescue equipment.

Kerala Floods

During the 2018 Kerala floods, several charity foundations provided essential relief. Khalsa Aid served over 3,000 meals daily through community kitchens in Kochi. Amma’s organization established a 24-hour helpline with 400 volunteers to coordinate rescue efforts. Habitat for Humanity, supported by Amazon, distributed relief materials worth ₹30 crore to over 116,000 families. The Indian Red Cross deployed hundreds of volunteers to assist displaced residents. ​
